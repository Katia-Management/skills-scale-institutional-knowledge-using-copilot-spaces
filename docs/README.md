# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a customer-first, iterative approach to project delivery that emphasizes clear ownership, data-informed decisions, and psychological safety. Our project management methodology guides cross-functional teams through a complete project lifecycle—from initial idea validation to post-release retrospectives—ensuring consistent, repeatable execution while fostering continuous improvement.

The framework is built on core principles of delivering small, testable increments with well-defined roles and responsibilities. Each project is owned by a Project Manager who coordinates delivery, schedules, and risk management, working closely with a Product Manager who defines outcomes and success metrics. Development teams collaborate through regular ceremonies including daily standups, sprint planning, and milestone reviews, supported by comprehensive documentation and communication protocols.

Risk management and quality assurance are integrated throughout the lifecycle. Teams maintain risk registers, conduct regular stakeholder updates, and follow standardized release processes with clear pre-release requirements and rollback procedures. The process emphasizes transparency through project boards, status reports, and decision logs, ensuring all stakeholders have visibility into progress and challenges.

Continuous learning is embedded in the culture through structured retrospectives after each sprint, release, or major milestone. Teams capture what went well, identify improvement opportunities, and commit to actionable changes. This iterative refinement of both products and processes helps OctoAcme maintain agility while scaling institutional knowledge across the organization.
